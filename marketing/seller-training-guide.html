Before you start — what you need
  • A smartphone with internet (this works right in your web browser — no app store needed).
  • Your phone number to receive a sign-in code by SMS.
  • A few clear product photos and your prices.
  • Your bank details if you want buyers to pay by EFT.

Getting started
Step 1

1 Log in & create your account

  1. Open yebosell.co.za and tap the menu, then Seller Login.
  2. Enter your phone number and tap continue. We send you a code by SMS.
  3. Type the code, then choose a 4-digit PIN. The PIN unlocks the app quickly next time — no SMS needed.
  4. Forgot your PIN later? Tap “Forgot PIN” and we'll text you a new code.
Tip: your phone number is your login. Use the same number every time.
Getting started
Step 2

2 Sign the seller agreement

  1. The first time you log in, the Seller Agreement appears. It explains the 5% platform fee and that YeboSell never holds your money — buyers pay you directly.
  2. Type your full name and your place of signing (e.g. Maseru), tick the box, and tap Sign & Continue.
  3. A signed PDF downloads to your phone automatically. Re-download it any time from Settings.
Tip: typing your name is your electronic signature — it's a real agreement, so read it once.
Set up your store
Step 3

3 Set up your store

  1. Go to Settings. Add your business name, a short description, and your logo.
  2. Add your pickup address (if buyers can collect) and your delivery fee if you deliver.
  3. Under bank details, add the account(s) buyers should pay into by EFT — you can add more than one.
  4. Tap Save.
Tip: a logo and a clear store name make buyers trust you faster.
Add products
Step 4

4 Add a product

  1. Open Products and tap Add Product.
  2. Enter the name, price and stock, and add a clear photo.
  3. Need sizes or colours? Add variants — each with its own stock.
  4. Set a prep time (days to get ready) so buyers see a delivery estimate. Tap Save.
Tip: bright, well-lit photos on a plain background sell best.
Edit products
Step 5

5 Edit a product

  1. In Products, tap the product you want to change — its details pop up.
  2. Tap Edit, change the price, photo, description or stock, and tap Save.
  3. To hide a product without deleting it, switch it to inactive. To remove it for good, tap Delete.
Tip: keep prices current — buyers pay the price shown at checkout.
Inventory
Step 6

6 Manage stock (inventory)

  1. Open Inventory to see every product's stock at a glance.
  2. Low-stock items are flagged so you can restock before you sell out.
  3. Update a count directly, or edit the product. Stock drops automatically when a buyer orders.
Tip: accurate stock avoids selling something you don't have.
Share & sell
Step 7

7 Share your store

  1. Tap Visit (or copy your store link) to open your storefront.
  2. Share that link on WhatsApp status, Facebook and Instagram, or send it to a customer directly.
  3. Anyone who opens the link can browse your products and place an order — no app needed.
Tip: pin your store link to your WhatsApp “About” and social bios.
Manage orders
Step 8

8 Receive & update an order

  1. When a buyer orders, you get a notification (turn on alerts when asked). Open Orders.
  2. Tap the order to see items, the customer and delivery details.
  3. Move it along the stages: New → Confirmed → Preparing → Ready / Out for delivery → Delivered.
  4. If you need more time, update the delivery timeline — the buyer is told automatically (a reason is required).
Tip: confirm new orders quickly — buyers love a fast first response.
Talk to buyers
Step 9

9 Message your buyer

  1. Inside an order, use the chat to message the buyer — they see it on their tracking page and by notification.
  2. Prefer WhatsApp? Tap Message on WhatsApp to open a chat with a ready-made note from your own number.
  3. Buyers can reply, send a payment proof, or ask questions — it all stays on the order.
Tip: keep it friendly and clear — good service brings repeat orders.

Get paid
Step 10

10 Get paid & platform fees

  1. Buyers pay you directly — by EFT to your bank details, or cash on collection/delivery.
  2. Once paid, mark the order Paid. The buyer follows everything on their tracking link.
  3. YeboSell charges a 5% platform fee per paid order (goods only). Your balance shows on the YeboSell Account card on your Dashboard; settle it when asked.
Tip: YeboSell never touches your money — the fee is billed separately, not deducted from sales.

The buyer's side & help
Step 11

11 What your customer sees

  1. Your customer opens your store link, adds items to a cart and checks out — entering their name, phone and delivery choice.
  2. They agree to the buyer terms and place the order. They get a tracking link they can save to their phone.
  3. From that link they follow the order status, chat with you, see your bank details, and can cancel before you dispatch.
Tip: encourage buyers to “Add to Home Screen” so they get order updates.
